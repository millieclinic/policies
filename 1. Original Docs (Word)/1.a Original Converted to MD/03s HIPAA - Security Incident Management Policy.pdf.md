Title:    Security Incident Management Policy

Policy #:  IT-009

Effective Date: Jul 5, 2022

Last Revised: May 2, 2022

I.

Scope

This  Policy  applies  to  all  facilities  and  locations  owned,  operated,  or  managed  by  Millie,  Inc.
(“Company”),  and  all  Company  personnel.   For  purposes  of  this  Policy,  the  term  “personnel”
includes all licensed professionals and staff who perform services on behalf of Company.

II.

Policy

a.

It is Company’s policy to ensure the prompt reporting of and response to security
incidents  in  order  to  prevent  the  occurrence  of  similar  incidents  in  the  future  and  safeguard
Company’s PHI.

b.

This  Policy  governs  the  reporting,  investigation  and  management  of  actual  and
suspected  security  incidents  that  may  compromise  the  security  of  PHI  or  cause  the  loss  of  or
damage to Company property.

III.

Procedure

a.

Incident Reporting.

i.

Subject  to  the  considerations  described  below,  all  personnel  shall  be
required to promptly report to the Chief Security Officer, or his or her designee, any observed or
suspected  (i)  security  weaknesses  or  threats  to  the  IT  System,  (ii)  violations  of  Company’s
security  policies,  and  (iii)  events  that  could  compromise  the  integrity,  confidentiality  and
availability  of  PHI.  If  the  security  incident  could  be  considered a Breach of HIPAA, the Chief
Security  Officer  shall  immediately  notify  the  Chief  Privacy  Officer.   Provided  below  is  a
non-exhaustive list of incidents that are to be reported to the Chief Security Officer or his or her
designee:

●  Actual or suspected loss or unauthorized disclosure of PHI;

●  Unauthorized access to or modification of PHI;

●  Unusual behavior of the IT System (e.g., missing files, frequent

crashes, misrouted messages);

●  Unauthorized use of the IT System;

●

Loss, theft or disclosure of a password or other access control
mechanism; or

●

Theft or loss of or material damage to Company property.

ii.

The  following  events may indicate a security threat to the IT System and

are to be reported to the Chief Security Officer:

●

Inability to log on to the IT System or any denial of service to a
Company account; or

●

Threatened or actual introduction of a virus.

iii.

Any personnel member who is uncertain as to whether a particular event is
reportable  should  contact  his  or  her  supervisor.  The Chief Security Officer shall investigate all
reports within 24 hours of receipt.

b.

Incident Response.

i.

The  Chief  Security  Officer,  in  conjunction  with  the  Chief  Compliance
Officer and Chief Privacy Officer, as appropriate, shall be responsible for managing Company’s
response  to  all  reported  security  incidents.  Upon  learning  of  a  security  incident,  the  Chief
Security  Officer  shall  conduct  a  preliminary  investigation  within  24  hours  of  the  reported
incident   for  purposes  of  ascertaining  whether  a  bona  fide  security  event  has  occurred.  If  a
security  incident  is verified, the Chief Security Officer shall promptly determine the nature and
scope of the incident and shall promptly report the matter to the Chief Privacy Officer and Chief
Compliance  Officer,  who  shall  report  the  incident  to the Chief Executive Officer and others as
appropriate  and  required.  The  Chief  Security  Officer  shall  then  designate  any other personnel,
third parties and other resources he or she deems necessary to effectively respond to the specific
incident  and  obtain  any  necessary  approvals  from  the  Chief  Compliance  Officer  or  Chief
Executive Officer.

ii.

Regardless  of  the  nature  and  scope  of  the  incident,  Company’s  response

shall be based upon the following order of priorities, as applicable:

●

●

Protection of human life and safety;

Protection  of  PHI  against  unauthorized  disclosure,  destruction  or
modification;

●

Prevention or mitigation of damage to Company’s assets;

●  Notification of any third parties that may be adversely affected by the
event;  however,  notification  shall  not  be  made  unless  and  until
approved by the General Counsel;

2

●  Minimizing  disruption  of  Company’s  computing  resources  and

processes; and

●

Reporting  the  incident  to  applicable  law  enforcement  agencies  and
insurance  carriers,  however, notification shall not be made unless and
until approved by the Chief Legal Officer.

iii.

Personnel  shall  not  discuss  any  events  pertaining  to  specific  security
incidents  with  other  individuals  (including  other  personnel),  except  as  permitted  under  this
Policy.  In  no  event  shall  personnel  attempt  to resolve a suspected security incident without the
authorization  of  the  Chief  Security  Officer  and  Chief  Compliance  Officer.  All  personnel  shall
cooperate  with  the  Chief  Security  Officer  and  Chief  Compliance  Officer  with  respect  to  all
aspects of the incident response process.

c.

Log.

i.

The  Chief  Security  Officer  shall  be  responsible  for  maintaining  a  log
containing  a  written  record  of  all  reported  incidents  and  the  details  and  events  relating  to  the
response process, including, as applicable:

●

●

●

●

The type of incident;

The date of the incident;

The date the incident was reported to the Chief Security Officer;

If  applicable,  the  date  the  incident  was  reported  to  Company  by  its

●

The manner in which the incident was identified;

●  All  internal  and  external  discussions  (including  the  parties  and  the

PHI

●  Any  law  enforcement  agencies  notified  and  the  dates  of  such

d.

Post-Incident Analysis.

i.

After  an  incident  has  been  satisfactorily  resolved or contained, the Chief
Security  Officer  shall  prepare  a  written  report  for  submission  to the Chief Privacy Officer and
Chief Compliance Officer, which shall include the following information, as applicable:

●  A detailed description of the incident;

●

●

The manner in which the incident occurred;

The  vulnerabilities  in  Company’s  security  protocols  and/or  the
operation of the IT System that were exploited (e.g., how unauthorized
access was gained and other relevant details);

●

The manner in which the incident was resolved or contained;

●  Whether existing security policies were adequate or require updating;

●  Any unresolved vulnerabilities;

●  An  inventory update for purposes of determining the extent of loss or

damage to property of Company; and

●  Any law enforcement agencies notified.

ii.

The  Chief  Compliance  Officer  and  Chief  Privacy  Officer  shall  forward

such report to the Chief Executive Officer, as appropriate.

e.

Annual Report.

i.

The Chief Security Officer and the Chief Privacy Officer will endeavor to
prepare  an  annual  privacy  and  security  report  for  submission  to  the  Chief  Executive  Officer,
which shall include the following items:

●  All  incidents  reported  and  responded  to  during  the  prior  year,

including breaches;

●

●

●

Security vulnerabilities that were corrected;

The costs associated with each security incident;

Identification of any existing security weaknesses; and

●

Recommendations  to  improve  Company’s  security defenses and limit
the frequency, damage and cost of future occurrences.
