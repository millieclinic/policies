## Part 9 — Risk Assessment (45 CFR §164.402 four-factor analysis)

A Breach is presumed unless the Privacy Officer documents a **low probability** that the PHI has been compromised based on the following four factors.

### Factor 1: Nature and extent of the PHI involved

- Describe the patient information involved: `_______________________`
- Were direct patient identifiers included (e.g., name, SSN)? **YES / NO**
- If no direct identifiers, is there a likelihood the PHI could be re-identified from context or by linking to other available information? **YES / NO**
- Is the PHI involved sensitive in nature (e.g., reproductive health, mental health, substance use, HIV)? **YES / NO** — If yes, describe: `_______________________`

### Factor 2: Unauthorized person who used the PHI or to whom disclosure was made

- Who accessed/used the information or to whom was it disclosed? `_______________________`
- Was the recipient someone with obligations to protect the privacy of PHI (e.g., another covered entity, attorney under privilege)? Explain. **YES / NO**
- Was the recipient someone with a reason to want to misuse the PHI (e.g., an employer, ex-partner, family member)? Explain. **YES / NO**

### Factor 3: Whether the PHI was actually acquired or viewed

- Can it be demonstrated that PHI was not actually acquired or viewed (e.g., a laptop was stolen but forensic analysis shows PHI was not accessed)? Explain. **YES / NO**

### Factor 4: Extent to which the risk to the PHI has been mitigated

- Have satisfactory assurances been received from the recipient that they destroyed the information and did not share the PHI? Explain and attach written attestation if available. **YES / NO**
