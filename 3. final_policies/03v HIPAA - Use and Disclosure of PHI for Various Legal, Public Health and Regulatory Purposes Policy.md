| **Title**: Use and Disclosure of PHI for Various Legal, Public Health, and Regulatory Purposes Policy | **Policy #**: L-002 |
| **Effective Date**: May 18, 2022 | **Last Revised**: May 2, 2022 |

1. **Scope**

This Policy applies to all facilities and locations owned, operated, or managed by Millie, Inc.(“Company”), and all Companypersonnel. For purposes of this Policy, the term “personnel” includes all licensed professionals and staff who perform services on behalf of Company.

1. **Policy**
2. In compliance with HIPAA, this Policy and procedure establishes the general rules which Company will follow when using and disclosing PHI for various legal, public health and regulatory purposes.
3. For the specific purposes described below, Company may use or disclose PHI without first obtaining the Individual’s written authorization and without providing the Individual with an opportunity to object. Many of the disclosures permitted under this Policy must be included in Company’s accountings of disclosures per Company’s Accounting of Disclosures Policy and must be logged accordingly.

Note**:** HIV-related information, genetic information, alcohol and/or substance abuse records and mental health records may receive additional confidentiality protections under state and federal laws which are not pre-empted by HIPAA and must be followed. Questions concerning disclosure involving these types of information should be discussed with your supervisor and/or discussed with the Chief Privacy Officer.

1. **Procedure**
2. *Uses and Disclosures Made for Health Oversight Activities*. Company may disclose PHI to a health oversight agency (e.g., the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services or the Florida Agency for Health Care Administration) for oversight activities authorized by law, including audits, civil and criminal investigations or proceedings, inspections, licensure or disciplinary actions.
3. *Exceptions*.Health oversight activities do not include investigations in which the Individual is the subject of an investigation that does not arise out of and is not directly related to (i) the receipt of healthcare; (ii) a claim for public benefits related to health; or (iii) qualification for, or receipt of, public benefits or services when an Individual’s health is integral to the claim. The rules governing disclosures for law enforcement purposes apply when the request is about an Individual for other purposes.

If Company is unable to determine whether a request for PHI is for a health oversight activity, Company should ask for an explanation of the purpose of the request and then consult with the Chief Privacy Officer**.**

1. *Uses and Disclosures for Public Health*. Company may disclose PHI:
2. *To Prevent or Control Disease*. If required by law for preventing or controlling disease, injury or disability and the conduct of public health surveillance, interventions or investigations.
3. *To Report Child Abuse or Neglect*. To the appropriate government authority authorized by law to receive reports of child abuse or neglect.
4. *To Fulfill Requirements of the Food and Drug Administration (FDA)*. For the purpose of activities related to the quality, safety or effectiveness of FDA-regulated products or activities. Such disclosures include, without limitation, those made for the following purposes:
   * + To collect or report adverse events, product defects or problems (including problems with the use or labeling of a product) or biological product deviations.
     + To track FDA-regulated products.
     + To enable product recalls, repairs, replacements or lookbacks (including locating and notifying people who have received products subject to recalls, withdrawals or lookbacks).
     + To conduct post-marketing surveillance.
5. *To Notify Persons of Exposure to Communicable Diseases*. If Company is authorized by law to notify such persons as necessary in the conduct of a public health intervention or investigation, e.g., for intervention of investigation of Covid-19 or tuberculosis.
6. *To Disclose Proof of Immunization to Schools*. If Company obtains agreement, which may be oral, from an Individual, guardian or other person acting *in* *loco* *parentis* for the Individual, or from the Individual if he or she is an adult or emancipated minor. Company should document any oral consent.
7. *Uses and Disclosures for Law Enforcement Purposes*.
8. *Disclosures Concerning Victims of Abuse, Neglect or Domestic Violence*.
   * + Except for reports of child abuse as described above, Company may disclose PHI about an Individual whom Company reasonably believes to be a victim of abuse, neglect or domestic violence, if the disclosure is to an appropriate government authority provided that:
       - The disclosure is required by law and complies with and is limited to the relevant requirements of such law (this must be required by law not merely authorized by law);
       - Agreed to by the Individual; or
       - Authorized by statute or regulations (this refers to disclosures that are permitted (though not required) by law); and:
         * Company reasonably believes that the disclosure is necessary to prevent serious harm to the Individual or other potential victims; or
         * The Individual is unable to agree due to incapacity, and a government authority represents that (a) the PHI sought is not intended to be used against the Individual, and (b) an immediate enforcement activity would be materially and adversely affected by waiting until the Individual is able to agree to the disclosure.
     + Company must promptly inform the Individual that such a report has been or will be made, except in circumstances where:
       - Company, in the exercise of its professional judgment, believes that informing the Individual would put the Individual at risk of serious harm.
       - Company would be required to inform a personal representative of the Individual, and Company reasonably believes that (a) such personal representative is responsible for the abuse, neglect or other injury, and (b) informing such person would not be in the best interests of the Individual.
9. *Disclosures to Law Enforcement Officials for Law Enforcement Purposes*.[[1]](#footnote-0) Disclosures may be made to law enforcement:

Note: PHI should not be disclosed pursuant to this section without first speaking to the Chief Privacy Officer, as appropriate.

* + - When required by law, including laws that require the reporting of certain types of wounds or other physical injuries.
    - In compliance with and as limited by the requirements of:
      * A court order, a court-ordered warrant or a summons issued by a judge.
      * An administrative request, including a subpoena or summons from an administrative agency, an investigative demand from an authorized body or a similar process authorized under law, provided that all three of the following requirements are met: (a) the PHI sought is relevant and material to a legitimate law enforcement inquiry; (b) the request is specific and limited in scope to the extent practicable in light of the purpose for which the PHI is sought; and (c) de-identified information could not reasonably be used.
    - For the purpose of identifying or locating a suspect, fugitive, material witness or missing person, provided that only the following PHI is disclosed:[[2]](#footnote-1)
      * Name and address;
      * Date and place of birth;
      * Social Security number;
      * ABO blood type and Rh factor;
      * Type of injury;
      * Date and time of Treatment;
      * Date and time of death, if applicable; and
      * A description of distinguishing physical characteristics, including height, weight, gender, race, hair and eye color and presence or absence of facial hair, scars and tattoos.
    - Except as otherwise noted herein, in response to a request about an Individual who is or is suspected to be a victim of a crime, other than a domestic abuse victim, if:
      * The Individual agrees to the disclosure; or
      * Company cannot obtain the Individual’s agreement because of incapacity or other emergency circumstances, provided that:
        + The law enforcement official states that (a) such PHI is needed to determine whether a person, other than the Individual, violated the law, and is not intended to be used against the Individual, and (b) immediate law enforcement activity would be materially adversely affected by waiting until the Individual-victim gains sufficient capacity to agree; and
        + Disclosure is in the best interest of the victim.
    - For the purpose of alerting an officer to the death of an Individual which Company suspects resulted from criminal conduct.
    - If Company believes in good faith that the PHI is evidence of criminal conduct that occurred on Company’s premises.
    - If Company provides emergency healthcare, other than in response to an emergency occurring on the premises of Company, and disclosure of the PHI is necessary to alert law enforcement to:
      * The commission and nature of a crime;
      * The location of the crime or victims of the crime; and
      * The identity, description and location of the perpetrator of the crime.

1. *Identification of a Deceased Person or Cause of Death*. Limited disclosures may be made to a coroner or medical examiner for the purpose of identifying a deceased person, determining a cause of death or the performance of other duties authorized by law.
2. *Funeral Arrangements*. Limited disclosures, including disclosures prior to the Individual’s death, may be made to funeral directors, consistent with applicable law, as necessary to carry out their duties with respect to the deceased person.
3. *Disclosures for Purposes of Workers’ Compensation*. Limited disclosures may be made as authorized by and to the extent necessary to comply with laws relating to workers’ compensation or other similar programs that provide benefits for work-related injuries or illness without regard to fault. Disclosures for this purpose should not be made without first contacting the Chief Privacy Officer.
4. *To Avert a Serious Threat to Health or Safety*. Limited disclosures may be made if consistent with applicable law, and if Company believes, in good faith, that the use or disclosure:
   * + Is necessary to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to the health or safety of a person or the public, and such disclosure is made to a person or persons reasonably able to prevent or lessen the threat.
     + Is necessary for law enforcement authorities to identify or apprehend an individual:
       - Who appears from all the circumstances to have escaped from a correctional institution or from lawful custody; or
       - Due to a statement made by an individual admitting participation in a violent crime which may have caused serious physical harm to the victim, if such disclosure is limited to the contents of the individual’s statement and provides only the PHI that can be disclosed when identifying a victim, as described above.
5. *Disclosures for Cadaveric Organ, Eye or Tissue Donation Purposes*. Company may use or disclose PHI to organ procurement organizations for the purpose of facilitating organ, eye or tissue donation or transplantation.
6. *Disclosures for Judicial and Administrative Proceedings*.The release of PHI in response to subpoenas or discovery requests that are not accompanied by a court order or Individual authorization is limited by state law and privileges. Therefore, personnel should not respond to such subpoenas unless authorized by the Chief Privacy Officer.
7. *Disclosures Made to Various Government Agencies*.Company may disclose PHI in the following situations:
8. *Uses and Disclosures for Military and Veteran Activities*. Uses and disclosures may be made concerning Individuals who are members of the armed forces or foreign military for activities deemed necessary by appropriate military command or foreign military authorities.
9. *Disclosures for National Security and Intelligence Activities*.Disclosure may be made to authorized federal officials for the performance of lawful intelligence, counterintelligence and other national security activities authorized by applicable law.
10. *Disclosures for Protective Services for the President and Others*. Disclosures may be made to authorized federal officials for the provision of protective services to the President or certain other executive persons or to foreign heads of state.
11. *Disclosures to Correctional Institutions and in Other Custodial Situations*. Disclosure may be made to a correctional institution or a law enforcement official, if the appropriate individual represents that PHI about an inmate or Individual in custody is necessary for:
    * + The provision of healthcare to such Individual.
      + The health and safety of such Individual or other inmates.
      + The health and safety of the officers or employees of or others at the correctional institution.
      + The health and safety of such individuals and officers or other persons responsible for the transporting of inmates or their transfer from one institution, covered entity or setting to another.
      + Law enforcement on the premises of the correctional institution.
      + The administration and maintenance of the safety, security and good order of the correctional institution.

Note: Because these are not routine occurrences, personnel should discuss the disclosure with the Chief Privacy Officer prior to making the disclosure.
