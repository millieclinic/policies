# Breach Notification — Sample Letter to Affected Individual

> Use this template under the direction of the Privacy Officer. All bracketed `[…]` fields must be completed before sending. Send by certified mail or hand-delivery to the individual's last known address. For minors or individuals lacking legal capacity, send to the parent or personal representative. See `incident-and-breach-response.md` §III.7.3 for content, method, and substitute-notice rules.

Although we do not believe that there is a likelihood of any additional unauthorized disclosures or misuse of your patient information, we are obligated under Federal law to let you know about the incident and to remind you to routinely monitor all of your accounts for misuse. `[If you want to offer credit monitoring or have any other mitigation suggestions, include here.]`

We assure you that we thoroughly investigated this incident and determined that the `[mistake appears to be a one-time error and not indicative of a systemic problem. Nevertheless, the applicable employee was counseled regarding this incident]` `[or include description of corrective actions that were taken]`.

We consider security of patient information to be of utmost importance and regularly review our security systems. ## Required content checklist (45 CFR §164.404(c))

Before sending, confirm the letter includes all of the following:

- [ ] Brief description of what happened, including date of the Breach and Date of Discovery (if known)
- [ ] Description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved (e.g., name, DOB, SSN, diagnosis)
- [ ] Steps the individual should take to protect themselves from potential harm
- [ ] Brief description of Millie's investigation, mitigation, and protective actions
- [ ] Contact information: toll-free number, email address, website, or postal address
- [ ] Plain language at an appropriate reading level
