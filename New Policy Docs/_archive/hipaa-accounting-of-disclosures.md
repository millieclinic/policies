| **Effective Date**: May 18, 2022 | **Last Revised**: May 2, 2022 |

1. **Scope**

This Policy applies to all facilities and locations owned, operated, or managed by Millie, Inc.(“Company”), and all Companypersonnel. For purposes of this Policy, the term “personnel” includes all licensed professionals and staff who perform services on behalf of Company.

1. **Policy**
2. It is Company’s policy to ensure that Company provides Individuals with an accounting of disclosures of their PHI in accordance with HIPAA and HITECH.
3. **Procedure**
4. *Disclosures Subject to an Accounting*.
5. Individuals may request an accounting of certain disclosures of their PHI made by Company for the six-year period immediately preceding the date of the request. All such requests must be directed to the Chief Privacy Officer.
6. The accounting provided by Company must include all disclosures of PHI other than those for the following purposes:

* To carry out Payment or Health Care Operations;
* To the Individual;
* Made pursuant to an authorization signed by the Individual that complies with the requirements of the HIPAA Privacy Rule;
* To federal officials for national security or intelligence purposes;
* To a correctional institution or law enforcement official that has custody of an Individual, upon a determination by the Chief Privacy Officer that such disclosure is not subject to an accounting under the HIPAA Privacy Rule; or
* To a health oversight or law enforcement agency if such agency notifies Company in writing that the provision of an accounting during a specified period would be reasonably likely to impede the agency’s activities.

1. Only disclosures to persons or entities outside of Company are subject to the accounting requirement. Internal use of PHI by Company personnel or Business Associates is not treated as a disclosure.
2. *Tracking Disclosures*.
3. Any personnel member who makes a disclosure of PHI that is subject to the accounting requirement must promptly inform the Chief Privacy Officer, in writing, of the disclosure. The written notice must state to whom the disclosure was made, what types of PHI were disclosed, the purpose of the disclosure and the date of the disclosure.
4. *Provision of an Accounting*.
5. Any request for an accounting submitted by an Individual received by a personnel member shall be transmitted within 24 hours of receipt to the Chief Privacy Officer. The Chief Privacy Officer will be responsible for preparing a response to the request.
6. The accounting provided by the Chief Privacy Officer to Individuals will include the following information for each disclosure subject to the accounting requirement:

* The date of the disclosure;
* The name of the recipient of the information and, if known, the recipient’s address;
* A brief description of the PHI disclosed; and
* A brief statement of the purpose of the disclosure.

1. In certain circumstances, if Company has made multiple disclosures of PHI, the accounting, in lieu of specifying the date of each disclosure, may state the frequency, schedule or number of disclosures, and the dates of the first and last disclosures. The circumstances in which such a statement may be provided include multiple disclosures to (i) the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services in order to comply with HIPAA, or (ii) other persons or entities to meet legal requirements, comply with government requests for information or satisfy other public interest purposes which have been determined by the Chief Privacy Officer to fall within 45 C.F.R. § 164.512.
2. *Time Frame for Providing Accounting*.
3. The Chief Privacy Officer will ensure that a response to an Individual’s request for an accounting is made no later than 60 days following receipt of the request (though an extension of 30 days may be requested if necessary).
4. *Fees*.
5. Company will not charge Individuals a fee for the first accounting requested in any 12-month period. For additional requests, the Chief Privacy Officer will inform the Individual in advance that the Individual will be charged a fee to cover Company’s reasonable costs associated with producing the accounting.
6. **References**

* 45 C.F.R. § 164.528
