| **Effective Date**: May 18, 2022 | **Last Revised**: May 2, 2020 |

1. **Scope**

This Policy applies to all facilities and locations owned, operated, or managed by Millie, Inc.(“Company”), and all Companypersonnel. For purposes of this Policy, the term “personnel” includes all licensed professionals and staff who perform services on behalf of Company.

1. **Policy**
2. This Policy establishes the right of Individuals to request amendments to their PHI in Company’s possession.
3. In accordance with HIPAA, with certain exceptions, an Individual has the right to request an amendment to his/her PHI maintained in a Designated Record Set (as such term is defined in Company’s Policy on Individuals’ Access to Their Information). Company will require that Individuals submit their requests to amend PHI in writing. All requests for amendments to PHI will be received and processed by Company’s Chief Privacy Officer or his/her designee for review. Company shall amend the record, if at all, in accordance with this Policy.
4. **Procedure**
5. *Timely Manner of Responding to Requests for Amendment*.
6. *60-Day Rule*.Company will act on an Individual’s written amendment request within 60 days of its receipt as described below, unless an extension is required.
7. *Extension Allowed*. If Company is unable to act on an amendment request within 60 days of its receipt, Company may extend the deadline for its response for up to 30 days. To receive an extension, Company must, within 60 days of its receipt of the written request, provide the Individual with a written statement of the reasons for the delay and the date by which Company will complete its processing of the request using the template attached as Exhibit A. Company may have only one such extension of time for any request for an amendment.
8. *Accepting the Amendment*.
9. If Company decides to accept a requested amendment, in whole or in part, Company will make the amendment by identifying the records that are affected by the amendment and then appending the amendment to the record or otherwise indicating the location of the amendment. The Chief Privacy Officer’s designee shall also notify the Individual using the letter template attached as Exhibit A. Company will inform the Individual that the amendment has been accepted.
10. Company will obtain the Individual’s agreement to have the amended PHI shared with other persons, when necessary. If the Individual agrees, Company will make reasonable efforts to provide a copy of the amendment within a reasonable time to (i) persons identified by the Individual as having received the PHI previously and needing a copy of the amendment, and (ii) persons, including Business Associates, that Company identifies as having the erroneous PHI and who may have relied, or could foreseeably rely, on such information to the detriment of the Individual. The Chief Privacy Officer will guide this process.
11. *Denying an Amendment Request*.
12. *Grounds for Denial*. Company may deny an Individual’s request for an amendment to his/her PHI under the following circumstances:
    * + Company did not create the PHI at issue (i.e., the records were created by another healthcare provider). However, if the Individual provides a reasonable basis to believe that the creator of the PHI is no longer available to act on the requested amendment, Company will address the request as if Company created the records.
      + The requested amendment is to a record which (i) is not a part of the Designated Record Set, or (ii) the Individual would otherwise not be allowed access.
      + Company determines the PHI in question is accurate and complete.
13. *Written Denial*. If Company denies a requested amendment, in whole or in part, Company will provide the Individual with a written denial within 60 days of its receipt of the Individual’s amendment request, using the template attached as Exhibit A. The written denial must comply with the following:
    * + Explain the basis for the denial.
      + Indicate the Individual’s right to submit a written statement disagreeing with the denial, and explain the process by which the Individual may file such a statement. If the Individual does submit a statement of disagreement, Company will include the statement with the records at issue. This statement of disagreement, or an accurate summary of the statement, will be included with any future disclosures of such records. Company may reasonably limit the length of a statement of disagreement, and Company may prepare a written rebuttal to an Individual’s statement of disagreement.
      + Explain that if the Individual does not submit a statement of disagreement, the Individual may request that with any future disclosures of the disputed health information, the Individual’s request for amendment and the denial will be included.
      + Include a description of the steps the Individual may take to complain to Company and/or to the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services.
14. *Amendments Made by Other Covered Entities*. If Company is informed by another covered entity (e.g., another provider or a health plan) of an amendment made by the covered entity to an Individual’s PHI, Company will include the amendment in any Designated Record Set maintained by Company for such Individual, to the extent applicable. Additionally, all Business Associates will be requested, upon receipt of a notice of an amendment, to incorporate any necessary amendments to any Designated Record Sets maintained by them on Company’s behalf.
15. **References**

* 45 C.F.R. §§ 164.502(g), 164.524 & 164.526
