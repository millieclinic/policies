1. **Scope**

This Policy applies to all facilities and locations owned, operated, or managed by Millie, Inc.(“Company”), and all Companypersonnel. For purposes of this Policy, the term “personnel” includes all licensed professionals and staff who perform services on behalf of Company**.**

1. **Policy**
2. It is Company’s policy to promote the proper handling and management of Company’s paper documents and records in order to safeguard the privacy and security of PHI contained in such materials.
3. It is Company’s policy that PHI generally should not be printed or maintained in paper format.
4. This Policy applies to the manner in which all paper documents containing PHI (“Protected Document”) are managed. If any document contains both PHI and other information, or if a personnel member is uncertain as to whether a document contains PHI, the document should be treated as though it contains PHI. All personnel are subject to this Policy.
5. **Procedure**
6. *Handling of Protected Documents*.
7. Company has adopted the following safeguards for protecting Protected Documents, each of which must be observed by personnel:

* Protected Documents may not be left unattended in any area to which the public may have access.
* All Protected Documents must be placed in folders, drawers, or face down on desks and workspaces when personnel are not actively working on such documents. At the end of each workday, all Protected Documents must be cleared from personnel’s desks and placed in a locked file cabinet or office if available.
* Personnel must promptly retrieve Protected Documents from printers, copiers, and fax machines.
* Corporate office personnel must dispose of Protected Documents by shredding them at one of Company’s secure shredding stations. Field-based personnel must also dispose of Protected Documents by shredding them.
* When mailing Protected Documents, personnel must take reasonable steps to ensure that they have the correct, up-to-date address. Protected Documents should not be left unsealed in outgoing mail bins or trays.
* Prior to gaining access to Protected Documents, all personnel must receive training in the use and disclosure of PHI, and Company must obtain evidence of such training.
* When handling Protected Documents, personnel must comply with all other applicable policies of Company, including Company’s Policy for the Use and Disclosure of PHI.

1. *Storage of Protected Documents*.
2. All Protected Documents that are maintained on-site outside of a personnel member’s office shall be stored in locked file cabinets or rooms that shall remain locked when not in use. Only authorized personnel shall have access to the keys or combinations to such cabinets and rooms.
3. *Destruction of Protected Documents*.
4. Protected Documents that do not need to be retained shall be destroyed at the end of each workday.
5. Protected Documents that have been stored by Company shall be destroyed by the Office Manager promptly when the retention period approved by Company has expired; provided, that the General Counsel has approved such destruction.
6. Protected Documents, including any surplus or draft copies, shall be destroyed only by shredding. If shredded on-site, such documents may be shredded only by authorized Company personnel.
7. Personnel involved in the selection process of off-site document shredding services on behalf of Company shall comply with the following requirements:

* Before any entity provides document shredding services, Company must execute a Business Associate Agreement with such entity.
* The entity must be appropriately insured.

1. To confirm that Protected Documents have been destroyed by vendors providing shredding services, the vendor must provide an executed certificate of destruction to Company. The certificate of destruction shall be maintained by the Chief Privacy Officer.
2. **References**
