# N/A Justifications (for assessor pushback)

One-sentence rebuttals for the N/A rows most likely to draw an assessor follow-up.

- **PCI DSS (all 10 rows):** Millie does not process, store, or transmit cardholder data — payments are outsourced to a third-party processor; we are a SAQ-A-style merchant with no card data in scope.
- **ISD IT Risk Assessment / Clinical Engineering / IoMT (row 64):** Millie operates two outpatient women's-health clinics using standard laptops, mobile devices, and SaaS — no medical devices connect to the IT network in a way that qualifies as IoMT.
- **ISD IT Risk Assessment / Telecommunications (row 65):** All telephony is delivered via RingCentral SaaS; no on-prem PBX, no special telecom interconnects.
- **SIG Lite E. Human Resources Security:** HR policies (background checks, performance reviews, sanctions) are administered through Gusto and reviewed by Millie's People Ops function; we do not maintain a separate security-team-owned HR policy at our headcount.
- **SIG Lite L.3 (Antitrust):** A 30-person clinic does not maintain a standalone antitrust program; antitrust matters, if they ever arose, would be handled through outside legal counsel.
- **SIG Lite L.4 / L.6 (Compliance & Ethics / Fraud):** Handled through outside legal counsel + HR (Gusto) and the whistleblower channels in the Safeguards Policy; no standalone Code of Conduct or fraud-prevention program at our size.
- **SIG Lite O (ESG, all 14 rows):** ESG / sustainability / DEI / modern-slavery / ethical-sourcing frameworks are not applicable to a small outpatient clinic and are not formally documented; the assessor can answer "not in scope."
- **SIG Lite P.9 (GLBA):** Millie is a healthcare provider, not a financial institution; GLBA does not apply.
- **SIG Lite P.12 (Registration accuracy every 3 months):** This terminology aligns with EU regulatory-authority registration; not applicable to a US-only clinic.
- **SIG Lite R (Artificial Intelligence):** Millie uses listed AI vendors (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Vertex, Heidi, Agno) all covered by BAAs where they touch PHI; AI use is governed by the BAA review process, the Minimum Necessary Rule Policy, and the Use & Disclosure Policy — a standalone AI policy is not warranted at our size.
